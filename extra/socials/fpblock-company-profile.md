## Core Offering

FP Block builds **application-specific blockchain infrastructure** using their proprietary framework **Kolme**, which gives clients:

- The performance and isolation of a dedicated chain
- Seamless interoperability with Ethereum, Solana, and Cosmos
- Full control without being cut off from the broader ecosystem

This is positioned as a "third path" — between building on congested shared public infra vs. spending millions on a custom L1/L2 from scratch.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

FP Block targets **teams where being wrong is more expensive than slowing down**:

- **Regret-Aware Builders** — Making irreversible system decisions with lasting financial/reputational/legal damage
- **Systemically Exposed Operators** — Running live systems where failures propagate to users and can't be reset
- **Regulated or Regulation-Bound Teams** — Systems that must withstand audits, enforcement, or legal challenge
- **Infrastructure Stewards** — Long-lived systems where ownership and accountability > feature velocity
- **AI-Accelerated Teams** — Shipping fast with AI but facing permanent architectural lock-in risk

### ICP Disqualifiers
- MVP-to-get-funded teams
- "We'll fix it later" mentality
- No clear downside owner
- Internal-only consequences
- Tool-first buyers optimizing stacks instead of incentives and trust

## Competitive Positioning

FP Block doesn't sell "blockchain development." They sell **engineered trust for systems that can't afford to fail**. The framing is deliberately industry-agnostic — if the problem sounds real without naming blockchain, it is real.
