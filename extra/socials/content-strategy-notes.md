## Content Pillars

### 1. The Weight of Permanence
Content about decisions that can't be undone — in infrastructure, in architecture, in business. This is Wes's core thesis and FP Block's core positioning.

**Example angles:**
- "The decisions that matter most are the ones you can't undo. In infrastructure, those are the ones most teams rush through."
- Commentary on real exploits/failures and what they reveal about irreversible architectural choices
- "AI makes it easy to ship. The question is: which of those decisions were you supposed to think about first?"

### 2. Invisible Technology
Wes's personal philosophy — the best tech disappears. Content about UX, adoption barriers, and why the industry overcomplicates things.

**Example angles:**
- Internet adoption analogy — nobody knows what TCP/IP is, and that's the point
- "If you have to explain your infrastructure to your users, you've already lost"
- Celebrating products/teams that made complex things feel simple

### 3. Builders Over Speculators
The industry is maturing. The next era belongs to teams shipping production systems, not chasing narratives.

**Example angles:**
- Enterprise adoption stories — "Enterprises aren't coming. They're already here."
- Contrast between conference hype and what's actually in production
- Celebrating teams that chose correctness over speed

### 4. Engineering Trust
Trust as an engineering problem, not a marketing one. How do you build systems that are trustworthy by design?

**Example angles:**
- "Trust isn't a feature. It's a property of how the system was built."
- What regulators actually look at vs. what teams think they look at
- The gap between "audited" and "actually secure"

### 5. AI + Infrastructure Risk
AI accelerates execution but creates new categories of lock-in risk. Wes is well-positioned to speak to this intersection.

**Example angles:**
- "AI makes the easy parts free. That's not the same as making the hard parts safe."
- Production readiness in an AI-assisted development world
- Which architectural decisions AI can't help you with

## Tone Calibration

| Attribute | X | LinkedIn |
|-----------|---|----------|
| Formality | Low-medium | Medium-high |
| Length | 1-5 tweets / 280 chars each | 3-6 paragraphs |
| Humor | Dry, observational OK | Minimal |
| Self-reference | Rare, indirect | Occasional, contextual |
| Call to action | Never explicit | Soft ("curious what others are seeing") |
| Jargon | Minimal — blockchain OK on X | Avoid where possible |
| Data/numbers | Use when punchy | Use when authoritative |
